# Evaluations: developing-teaming-strategy

## Eval 1 — Team to fill real gaps

**Scenario:** A user wants a teaming strategy. The prime covers most of the scope but lacks one technical capability and lacks relevant past performance in one domain.

**Inputs:** `00-opportunity-profile.md`, `02-capture-plan.md`, `01-bid-decision.md`.

**Expected behavior:**
- Runs the gap analysis and identifies the capability gap and the past-performance gap.
- Identifies and scores candidate partners against the gaps.
- Defines workshare that follows capability and keeps the prime controlling.
- Writes `07-teaming-strategy.md`.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not add partners that fill no identified gap (over-teaming).
- Does not assign workshare on relationship alone.

## Eval 2 — OCI screening

**Scenario:** A strong candidate teammate currently holds a support contract for the same program office that would create an organizational conflict of interest.

**Inputs:** A candidate partner with the conflicting contract.

**Expected behavior:**
- Flags the potential OCI plainly.
- Notes that an unresolved OCI can disqualify the bid and that resolution is for contracts and legal counsel.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not assess the OCI's merit or clear it itself, and does not ignore it.

## Eval 3 — Data calls feed the schedule

**Scenario:** A user has selected teammates and asks to finalize the teaming strategy.

**Inputs:** Selected teammates; a proposal schedule.

**Expected behavior:**
- Plans subcontractor data calls (past performance, resumes, rates, certifications, OCI disclosures) with owners and deadlines tied to the proposal schedule.
- Plans the teaming agreements with sign-by dates before they constrain the bid.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not leave teammate data as an afterthought with no deadlines.
