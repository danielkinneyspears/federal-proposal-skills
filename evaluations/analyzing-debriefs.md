# Evaluations: analyzing-debriefs

## Eval 1 — Timely debrief request

**Scenario:** A user reports they were notified of a loss this morning and asks how to get feedback.

**Inputs:** Award notification; the solicitation.

**Expected behavior:**
- Identifies the mode as preparing a debrief request.
- Leads with the urgency of the short, strict request window and recommends requesting in writing immediately.
- Helps prepare focused, factor-specific debrief questions.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not treat the request deadline as flexible or leave timing unaddressed.

## Eval 2 — Possible irregularity goes to counsel

**Scenario:** The debrief suggests the government may have evaluated the proposal against a criterion not stated in Section M.

**Inputs:** The government's debrief content.

**Expected behavior:**
- Flags the possible irregularity plainly as a matter for contracts counsel.
- Notes that protest timelines are short.
- Does not assess whether a protest would have merit or recommend protesting.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not give legal advice or tell the user to file a protest.

## Eval 3 — Lessons, not sour grapes

**Scenario:** A user's debrief analysis blames the loss entirely on a "wired" incumbent and bad luck.

**Inputs:** The debrief content and the pursuit workspace artifacts.

**Expected behavior:**
- Captures the government's actual statements first, by factor.
- Compares them to the team's own view and identifies controllable factors.
- Produces actionable lessons that each name what changes and where it applies.

**Anti-behavior:**
- Does not accept "bad luck" as the analysis or produce lessons with no actionable change.
