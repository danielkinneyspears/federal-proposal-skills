# Question and Amendment Control

How to run the question log, submit formal questions to the contracting officer,
and analyze the impact of amendments. After the RFP releases, this is the only
sanctioned channel of communication with the government — and it is a channel
many teams manage poorly.

## Contents
- The post-release communication rule

- Drafting formal questions
- Processing government answers
- Amendment impact analysis

## The post-release communication rule

Once the RFP releases, communication with the customer is normally restricted to
the contracting officer through the formal question-and-answer process.
Pre-release customer engagement is over (see `planning-capture`). Every question
the team has now goes through one channel, by one deadline, and every answer the
government gives goes to all offerors. Managing that channel well is an
operational discipline.

## The question log

The question log is the running record of every question the team has for the
government and every answer received. For each entry:

- The question, in clear and answerable form.
- Where it came from (which volume, which requirement, which ambiguity — often a
  flagged item from `shredding-solicitations`).
- Status: draft, submitted, answered.
- The government's answer and the date received.
- The impact: which requirements, artifacts, or sections the answer affects.

The log feeds two ways: questions out to the government, and answers back into
the compliance matrix and the affected sections.

## Drafting formal questions

Questions to the contracting officer are submitted in writing by the deadline
Section L sets. Effective questions:

- **Are specific.** Cite the section and paragraph. A vague question gets a
  vague or unhelpful answer.
- **Are answerable.** Ask something the government can actually clarify — an
  ambiguity, a conflict, a missing instruction (often the L/M conflicts and gaps
  flagged during the shred).
- **Do not reveal strategy.** A question can telegraph an offeror's approach or
  weakness to every competitor, because answers are shared. Ask what the team
  needs clarified without exposing how it intends to win.
- **Do not argue.** The question process clarifies the solicitation; it is not a
  forum to lobby for a change favorable to one offeror.

Prioritize questions: a genuine ambiguity that blocks a compliant response
matters more than a minor preference. Submit by the deadline; questions after it
may not be answered.

## Processing government answers

Government answers arrive as an amendment or a Q&A attachment and apply to all
offerors. For each answer:

1. Log it against the question.
2. Determine what it changes — a clarified requirement, a new instruction, a
   resolved ambiguity.
3. Update the compliance matrix and notify the owners of every affected section.
4. If the answer changes the win approach, flag it to the capture lead.

An answer the team logs but does not act on is worse than an unasked question.

## Amendment impact analysis

An amendment is a formal change to the solicitation. It can change requirements,
Section L, Section M, the due date, or the contract terms. Every amendment gets
an impact analysis:

1. **Acknowledge** the amendment as the solicitation directs — a missed
   acknowledgment can make the proposal non-compliant.
2. **Diff it.** Identify exactly what changed, section by section.
3. **Re-shred the affected sections.** Do not assume a change is small; have
   `shredding-solicitations` update the compliance matrix for the affected
   requirements, preserving requirement IDs.
4. **Assess the schedule.** If the amendment moved the due date, rebuild the
   schedule backward from the new date. If it added requirements late, assess
   whether the schedule still holds.
5. **Notify owners.** Every section affected by the amendment gets routed to its
   author with the specific change.
6. **Re-examine the bid.** A large, late amendment can change the opportunity
   enough to warrant re-running `qualifying-opportunities` at the bid-validation
   gate.

An amendment absorbed late, or absorbed partially, is one of the most
preventable ways to lose — or to be found non-compliant.
