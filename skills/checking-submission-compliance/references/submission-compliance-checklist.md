# Submission Compliance Checklist

The master checklist for the final pre-submission check, and the common causes
of proposal rejection. Every item is verified against the actual proposal files
and the actual solicitation — not assumed.

## Why proposals get rejected

A federal proposal can be eliminated without evaluation for failing to conform
to the solicitation's instructions. The most common causes are entirely
preventable: an over-length volume, a missed certification, price information in
a volume that bars it, the wrong file format, a late submission, an inactive
SAM.gov registration. None of these reflect the quality of the offer. All of
them lose. This checklist exists to catch them while there is still time.

## Format compliance

For each volume, verify against the Section L rules:

- **Page count** — at or under the limit. Confirm what counts: covers, tables of
  contents, dividers, graphics, glossaries, and appendices may or may not count;
  Section L says which. Count only what counts, against the limit.
- **Font and point size** — the specified typeface and minimum point size,
  including text inside graphics and tables, which often have their own minimum.
- **Margins and spacing** — page margins, line spacing, and any
  characters-per-inch or lines-per-page rule.
- **Page size and orientation** — paper size; whether landscape pages are
  allowed and how they count.
- **Headers, footers, and proprietary markings** — required content and
  placement; consistent proprietary legends if used.

An over-length volume is the single most common format failure. The government
may refuse to read pages past the limit, or reject the volume.

## File compliance

- **File format** — the required format (commonly PDF), and whether the PDF must
  be searchable, unlocked, or virus-scanned.
- **File naming** — the exact naming convention. Submission portals frequently
  require precise file names and reject mismatches.
- **File size** — per-file and total size limits, especially for portal uploads.
- **One-volume-per-file rules** — whether each volume must be a separate file.
- **Page numbering and bookmarks** — any required numbering scheme or PDF
  bookmark structure.

## Volume separation

- **Price separation** — cost and price information must appear only in the
  volume(s) the solicitation permits. Price bleeding into the technical or
  management volume is a serious, common error; some evaluations are structured
  so technical evaluators must not see price at all.
- **Volume independence** — where Section L requires volumes to be independently
  evaluable, confirm a volume does not rely on content in another.
- **Cross-reference rules** — whether cross-references between volumes are
  allowed or prohibited.

## Forms, representations, and certifications

- **Cover/solicitation form** — the SF 33, SF 1449, or other cover form,
  completed and signed by an authorized representative.
- **Section K** — all representations and certifications completed; confirm
  whether they are done in SAM.gov, in the proposal, or both.
- **Solicitation-specific certifications** — any certifications unique to this
  solicitation (small business, OCI, supply-chain, and others).
- **Amendments** — every amendment acknowledged as the solicitation directs.
- **SAM.gov registration** — active and current. An inactive registration can
  prevent award.
- **Required attachments** — letters of commitment, teaming agreements,
  subcontracting plan, and any other attachment Section L requires.

## Submission mechanics

- **Method** — portal, email, or physical delivery, exactly as Section L states.
- **Destination** — the correct portal, email address, or physical address; the
  correct contracting officer or point of contact.
- **Registration** — any portal account or pre-registration completed in
  advance, not discovered at the deadline.
- **Deadline** — the due date, the time, and the time zone. Confirm all three.
- **Confirmation** — how delivery is confirmed (a portal receipt, a read
  receipt).
- **Margin** — the plan submits well before the deadline. Late is late; the FAR
  rules on late proposals are strict and rarely forgiving.

## Mandatory requirement closeout

Walk `10-compliance-matrix.md` end to end. Every requirement marked mandatory
must have status "Compliant". A mandatory requirement still "At risk", "Drafted",
or "In draft" at submission time is an open exposure — surface it as a go/no-go
issue.

## The submission plan

The check ends with a confirmed plan: who uploads or delivers the proposal, from
where, when (with margin), and how delivery will be confirmed and recorded. The
plan names a backup person and a backup method in case the primary path fails.
